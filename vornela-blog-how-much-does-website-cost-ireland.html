How Much Does a Website
Cost in Ireland?
The 2026 Honest Guide

March 18, 2026 By Vornela Systems

How much does a website cost in Ireland? The honest answer is: anywhere from free to €20,000 or more — and the difference between a €500 site and a €5,000 site is not always what you'd expect. This guide breaks down exactly what you get at each price point, what you actually need depending on your business, and how to avoid the most expensive mistake Irish business owners make when buying a website.

What Website Prices in Ireland Actually Look Like

Here's a straightforward breakdown of the price tiers and what they include:

DIY website builders: €0–€30/month

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and Webador let you build a website yourself using drag-and-drop templates. There's no upfront build cost, just a monthly subscription. If you're comfortable with basic software and have the time to build and maintain it yourself, this is a legitimate option for very early-stage businesses.

The limitations: these sites are template-based and difficult to differentiate from thousands of similar-looking sites, their SEO capabilities are limited compared to a custom-built site, and you're spending your own time building and managing something that isn't your core skill. They're also not designed to convert visitors into enquiries — they're designed to be easy to build.

Freelancer or basic agency: €800–€2,500

A freelance web designer or small agency will build you a basic website — usually 5 to 8 pages — using a WordPress or similar CMS template. You get a real website you own, with your branding, content, and contact forms. This tier is appropriate for businesses that need a credible online presence but don't rely heavily on the website for lead generation.

Watch out for: limited post-launch support, no SEO work beyond basic setup, and the risk of a template-based design that doesn't convert well. A 2024 survey of Irish SMEs found that 62% of businesses who chose the cheapest web design option paid for a complete rebuild within 18 months. Often the cheapest option ends up being the most expensive over two years.

Professional small business website: €2,500–€6,000

This is the tier where most Irish SMEs who want their website to actively generate leads should be operating. A professionally designed website in this range includes:

  • Custom design built around your brand and target customer
  • Conversion-focused layout — clear CTAs, prominent contact options, trust signals
  • On-page SEO including proper meta tags, schema markup, page structure, and local SEO signals
  • Mobile optimisation (essential — 57% of Irish web traffic is mobile)
  • Gallery, reviews section, FAQs, and service pages that rank independently
  • Google Analytics and Search Console setup
  • Post-launch support period

A website in this range, built properly and connected to an active Google Business Profile, will typically generate consistent inbound enquiries within 3 to 6 months of launch.

Full-featured business website: €6,000–€12,000

For businesses with multiple services, multiple locations, or a need for e-commerce, blog content systems, booking integrations, or custom functionality. This tier typically involves a larger agency, a more involved design process, and a more comprehensive SEO strategy built in from the start.

Enterprise/e-commerce: €12,000+

Large-scale online stores, membership sites, or enterprise platforms with custom integrations, CRM connections, and ongoing development needs. Not relevant to most Irish SMEs but worth knowing where the ceiling is.

There's a Grant for This in Ireland

Most Irish business owners don't know this: the Local Enterprise Office (LEO) Trading Online Voucher provides eligible businesses with up to €2,500 towards the cost of building or improving a website — with the business matching 50% of the total cost.

That means a €5,000 professional website effectively costs you €2,500 out of pocket. A €3,000 website costs you €1,500.

Eligibility requirements:

  • 10 or fewer employees
  • Annual turnover under €2 million
  • Trading for at least 6 months
  • Based in Ireland

Applications go through your local LEO office. The scheme is regularly open and has helped thousands of Irish businesses get professional websites at significantly reduced cost. If you qualify, there's no reason not to use it.

What Determines How Much You Should Spend

The right budget for your website depends on one thing: how much work you want the website to do for your business.

A simple test: If a new customer from your website is worth €300, and your website generates 5 new customers per month, that's €1,500/month or €18,000/year in revenue directly attributable to the site. A €4,000 website that does this pays for itself in under 3 months. A €500 website that generates zero enquiries costs nothing upfront and everything over time.

The businesses that get the worst value from their websites are those that spend the minimum and wonder why nobody calls. The businesses that get the best value treat the website as a long-term asset, invest appropriately for what they want it to do, and connect it properly to SEO and Google Business Profile from day one.

Questions to Ask Any Web Design Company in Ireland

Before committing to any web design company, ask:

  • "Can I see 5 websites you've built for Irish businesses similar to mine?" — If they can't show you relevant examples, they're not the right fit
  • "What's included after launch and what costs extra?" — Ongoing support, hosting, content updates, and SEO maintenance are often sold separately
  • "What will you do for SEO and how will I rank on Google?" — Vague answers here usually mean nothing substantive is included
  • "Who owns the website when it's built?" — You should always own your own site and have full access to it
  • "Can I speak to a client you've built a site for in the last 6 months?" — A company that builds good work is happy to share references

What a Good Website Actually Needs to Do

Regardless of what you spend, a website that generates leads for an Irish business needs to do six things:

  1. Pass the 5-second test — a visitor knows within 5 seconds what you do, where you work, and how to contact you
  2. Look credible on mobile — more than half of your visitors are on a phone
  3. Show real work — photos of actual jobs build more trust than stock images
  4. Display genuine reviews — with names, star ratings, and specific details
  5. Make contact effortless — a visible phone number, a form that works, a WhatsApp link if you use it
  6. Be findable on Google — proper meta tags, schema markup, and local SEO signals that connect to your Google Business Profile

A website that does these six things consistently will outperform a more expensive one that doesn't.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a website cost in Ireland in 2026?

Website costs in Ireland range from free (DIY builders) to €300–€800 for basic templates, €1,500–€3,500 for a professionally designed small business site, €3,500–€8,000 for a full-featured site with SEO and conversion optimisation, and €10,000+ for large e-commerce or enterprise websites. For most Irish SMEs looking for a website that generates leads, the €2,500 to €5,000 range is where the best value sits.
Can I get a grant for a website in Ireland?

Yes. The Local Enterprise Office (LEO) Trading Online Voucher provides eligible Irish businesses with up to €2,500 towards the cost of a website, with the business matching 50% of the total cost. Eligibility: 10 or fewer employees, annual turnover under €2 million, trading for at least 6 months, based in Ireland. Applications go through your local LEO office.
What is included in the price of a website in Ireland?

A professionally built website typically includes design and development, content support, mobile optimisation, basic on-page SEO, a contact form, and Google Analytics integration. Hosting and domain registration are usually ongoing annual costs paid separately. Always confirm what's included before signing — some agencies charge extra for SEO, support, and content.
How long does it take to build a website in Ireland?

A professionally designed website for an Irish small business typically takes 3 to 8 weeks from kickoff to launch. Simple 5-page brochure sites can be done in 2 to 3 weeks. Larger sites with e-commerce, multiple service pages, or custom functionality typically take 6 to 12 weeks.
What should I look for when choosing a web design company in Ireland?

Look for: a portfolio of real sites they've built, case studies with results not just aesthetics, clarity about what's included in the price, understanding of Irish SEO and local search, and post-launch support. Always ask to speak to a previous client. A company unwilling to share references is a red flag.
