The €126,000 Problem:
What Missed Calls Are
Actually Costing Your Business

April 10, 2026 By Vornela Systems

You're on a roof in Wicklow at 11am on a Tuesday. Your phone rings, you can't answer, and the call goes to voicemail. The caller hangs up without leaving a message — because they almost never do — and 30 seconds later they're ringing the next roofer on the list.

That's one missed call. It feels like nothing. A minor inconvenience, quickly forgotten.

But that call happens again on Wednesday. And Thursday. And twice the following week. And every week for the next twelve months.

Add it up, and you're looking at a six-figure hole in your annual revenue. Not from a single bad decision, not from a failed marketing campaign — from something as small and invisible as not answering the phone at the wrong moment.

The Maths That Most Business Owners Haven't Done

Let's build the number from the ground up, using figures that are conservative rather than alarming.

The average busy trades business or service SME in Ireland misses somewhere between 8 and 15 calls per week. That's not unusual — tradespeople are on site, in meetings, doing estimates, driving between jobs. Being unavailable is part of the work. Let's use 10 missed calls per week as our baseline.

Research consistently shows that 8 in 10 callers who reach voicemail won't leave a message. They hang up and move on. So of those 10 missed calls, let's assume 8 are gone the moment the voicemail kicks in — they won't call back, and you have no way of reaching them.

Of those 8 unreachable callers, roughly 50 to 60% would have converted had you answered. That's an accepted conversion rate for inbound enquiry calls — people calling your business specifically have already done some level of research and have intent to hire. Let's use the conservative end: 50% conversion rate. That's 4 lost customers per week.

Now apply an average job value. For a plumber or electrician, average job values typically sit between €350 and €700. For a builder or roofer, significantly higher. Let's use a very conservative €480 average.

The calculation:
10 missed calls/week × 80% don't call back = 8 gone
8 × 50% would have converted = 4 lost jobs/week
4 × €480 average job value = €1,920/week
€1,920 × 52 weeks = €99,840 per year

Push the missed call rate to 12 per week, or the average job value to €550, and you're over €126,000 in annual revenue walking out the door through unanswered phones.

That's not a marketing problem. That's not a pricing problem. That's a first-contact problem — and it's entirely fixable.

Why Callers Don't Wait

Understanding why missed calls become lost leads matters if you want to solve the problem properly. The instinct is to assume callers will try again later. The data shows otherwise.

A study published in the Harvard Business Review found that companies contacting leads within 5 minutes of an enquiry were 100 times more likely to convert than those who responded after 30 minutes. After an hour, the advantage was virtually gone. After a day, the lead was functionally dead.

For a phone call, the clock starts the second it goes unanswered. The caller's next action — dialling a competitor — takes about 10 seconds.

This is compounded by search behaviour. When someone searches "plumber near me" and calls the first result, they're in solution-seeking mode. They have a problem they want solved today. They'll call down the list until someone picks up. The business that answers (or responds instantly) gets the job. The businesses that don't answer share the remainder.

The Gap Irish Businesses Aren't Seeing

Here's what makes this particularly significant for Irish SMEs right now: virtually no Irish business has a system in place to handle missed calls automatically.

In the US and UK, missed call automation — where a text message is sent automatically within seconds of a call going unanswered — has become standard practice among businesses that care about lead recovery. In Ireland, it's almost unheard of. Which means the business that sets this up first in their local area has a meaningful, lasting advantage over every competitor who doesn't.

78% of customers say they will do business with the first company to respond to their enquiry. If you're the only business in your area sending an instant follow-up text when you miss a call, you're winning a significant percentage of leads that your competitors lose by default.

What Missed Call Automation Actually Does

The mechanics are straightforward. When a call comes in and goes unanswered — whether you declined it, were already on a call, or simply didn't hear it — a trigger fires and an automated message is sent from your business number to the caller's mobile. This happens in under 15 seconds.

The message is written to sound like you. Not a corporate auto-reply. Something like:

"Hi, sorry I missed your call — I'm on a job at the moment. I'll ring you back this afternoon between 3 and 5. In the meantime, feel free to reply here with what you need and I'll come back to you. — Seán, Seán Murphy Plumbing"

That message does four things at once:

  • Acknowledges the caller so they know they've been seen — removing the feeling of being ignored
  • Sets a specific expectation for when you'll call back, so they don't feel the need to dial the next number
  • Opens a two-way channel — now they can reply with their details and you pick up a warm lead, not a cold call
  • Brands the interaction — your name, your number, your voice

In practice, a large percentage of callers who receive this message will reply rather than call a competitor. You've turned a missed call into an active conversation without lifting a finger.

Businesses that implement missed call automation consistently report recovering between 30 and 60% of calls that would otherwise have gone silent. At the conservative end of that range — 30% recovery — applied to our earlier calculation:

Previously losing: ~4 jobs per week from missed calls
30% recovery = ~1.2 additional jobs per week retained
1.2 × €480 × 52 weeks = ~€29,952 additional annual revenue

Frequently Asked Questions

What is missed call automation?

Missed call automation is a system that detects when an incoming call goes unanswered and automatically sends a personalised text message to the caller within seconds. The message acknowledges the missed call, sets a callback expectation, and invites the customer to reply — keeping the lead alive rather than sending them to a competitor.
How quickly does the automated text send after a missed call?

Most systems send the follow-up message within 8 to 15 seconds of the call going unanswered. This near-instant response is critical — research shows customers are 100 times more likely to convert when contacted within 5 minutes of their initial enquiry versus 30 minutes later.
Does the automated message feel impersonal?

Not when set up correctly. The message is written in your voice, with your name, and sounds like something you would send yourself. For example: "Hi, sorry I missed your call — I'm on a job right now. I'll ring you back this afternoon. Feel free to reply here with what you need." Most customers don't realise it's automated.
How much does missed call automation cost to set up in Ireland?

Most Irish SMEs can have a full missed call automation system live for a one-time setup fee plus a low monthly running cost. Given that recovering just one or two leads per month typically covers the entire annual cost, the ROI is usually achieved within the first week of the system going live.
What kinds of businesses benefit most from missed call automation?

Any Irish business where phone enquiries are a primary lead source: tradespeople (plumbers, electricians, roofers, builders), healthcare providers, cleaning companies, salons, estate agents — any service business where each new customer is worth €200 or more and where being on-site or in meetings means missed calls are unavoidable.
