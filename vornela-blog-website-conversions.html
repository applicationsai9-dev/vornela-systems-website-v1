Your Website Gets Visitors —
But Is It Actually Converting Them?

March 18, 2026 By Vornela Systems

The most common frustration we hear from Irish SME owners goes something like this: "We get traffic — Google Analytics shows hundreds of visitors every month — but the phone barely rings and the contact form is nearly empty."

Traffic without conversions is one of the most expensive problems a business can have. It means every euro you've spent on SEO, Google Ads, social media, or word of mouth is bringing people to your door — and then watching them leave without doing anything.

The problem is almost never the traffic itself. It's what happens when that traffic lands on your website.

The 5-Second Test

Before getting into the specific fixes, run this test on your own website: open your homepage and ask yourself — within 5 seconds, could a complete stranger understand what your business does, where you operate, and what they should do next?

If the answer is anything other than a clear yes, you're losing leads before they've had a chance to become customers. Research consistently shows that the majority of website visitors decide whether to stay or leave within 3 to 5 seconds of landing on a page. If your message isn't instantly clear, they're gone.

Six Silent Conversion Killers

1. A Headline That Talks About You, Not Them

Most Irish business websites open with a headline focused on the business itself: "Welcome to Murphy Electrical" or "Quality Plumbing Services in Dublin." These headlines fail the 5-second test immediately because they communicate nothing about what's in it for the visitor.

A converting headline answers the customer's unspoken question — "What's here for me?"

Compare:

  • Weak: "Murphy Electrical — Dublin's Leading Electrician"
  • Strong: "Murphy Electrical — Fast, Reliable Electrical Work in Dublin. Fully Insured. Free Quotes."

The second version immediately communicates the benefit (fast, reliable), the location (Dublin), a trust signal (fully insured), and a low-friction next step (free quotes). That's four conversion factors in one headline.

2. No Clear Call to Action Above the Fold

"Above the fold" refers to the part of your website visible without scrolling. For most visitors on mobile — which accounts for 65–75% of Irish SME website traffic — this is an area roughly 600 pixels tall. If there isn't a clear, prominent button or phone number in that space, you're relying on visitors to scroll and hunt for a way to contact you. Most won't.

Every page should have a single, dominant call to action in a position where it can't be missed. For a service business, this is usually "Get a Free Quote" or "Call Now." The button should be in a contrasting colour that stands out from the rest of the design, and it should appear in the first screen of content on every device.

3. Slow Load Times — Especially on Mobile

Google data shows that a 1-second delay in mobile page load time reduces conversions by up to 20%. For every additional second a page takes to load, the probability of a visitor leaving without doing anything increases by roughly 32%.

Most Irish SME websites score poorly on Google PageSpeed Insights — often in the 30–50 range out of 100 on mobile. This is one of the easiest wins available because it affects both conversion rate and your Google search ranking simultaneously.

The most common speed issues on SME sites:

  • Unoptimised images (a 4MB photo that should be 80KB)
  • Too many third-party scripts loading on every page (chat widgets, trackers, fonts)
  • Render-blocking resources preventing the page from displaying quickly
  • No caching configured on the server

Quick check: Go to pagespeed.web.dev, enter your website URL, and select Mobile. A score below 70 means speed is actively hurting your conversions and your search ranking.

4. No Social Proof — Or Proof That's Hard to Find

Before most customers contact a business they've never used, they want to know that other people have — and that those people were happy. Reviews, testimonials, and case studies are the online equivalent of a personal recommendation, and they're one of the highest-impact elements on any service business website.

The common mistakes Irish SMEs make with social proof:

  • Burying reviews at the bottom of the page where visitors who haven't scrolled far won't see them
  • Using generic testimonials with no names, no photos, and no specific details ("Great service, highly recommend" — from Anonymous)
  • Not showing how many reviews you have in total (a business with 80 five-star reviews is far more compelling than one showing 3)
  • Not displaying review dates (a review from 4 years ago carries almost no weight with modern customers)

Real names, real photos or initials, specific job details, and recent dates — these are the four elements that make social proof actually work. Position it prominently: after your services section, not buried in the footer.

5. A Contact Form With Too Many Fields

Every additional field on a contact form reduces the probability of completion. Research by Hubspot found that reducing a contact form from 11 fields to 4 fields increased conversions by 120%.

For most Irish service businesses, a converting contact form needs three things: name, phone number, and a brief message about what they need. That's it. Adding fields for email, address, preferred date, service type, budget range, and "how did you hear about us" — all at once — turns an enquiry form into a job application, and most visitors will abandon it.

If you need more information, collect it after the initial contact — not before. The goal of the form is to get a conversation started, not to pre-qualify the lead completely before you've spoken to them.

6. Not Enough Trust Signals Visible on First Load

Trust signals are specific, verifiable facts that give visitors confidence in your business before they've spoken to you. The key word is specific. Vague claims like "years of experience" and "quality service" have almost no impact because they're used by every business and are impossible to verify.

Trust signals that actually work:

  • Specific credentials: "RGI Registered Gas Installer" or "Safe Electric Registered Electrical Contractor" — these are verifiable and meaningful to customers
  • Specific numbers: "Over 600 jobs completed in Dublin" is 10x more convincing than "hundreds of satisfied customers"
  • Insurance and guarantees: "Fully insured — public liability and employer's liability" and "All work guaranteed in writing"
  • Real photos: Stock photos undermine credibility. Photos of your actual work, your actual van, and your actual team build far more trust

The Most Valuable Fix Is Always the Clearest One

If your website is getting traffic but not converting, the answer is almost never "add more content." It's almost always one of the six issues above — and fixing even one of them can double or triple your enquiry rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good conversion rate for an Irish SME website?

For a service business website in Ireland, 2–5% (2 to 5 enquiries per 100 visitors) is a reasonable target. Many SME sites convert below 1%, meaning 99% of visitors leave without taking action. Improving conversion rate is often more valuable than increasing traffic because it improves ROI from all your existing marketing spend.
Why is my website getting traffic but no enquiries?

The most common reasons: the headline doesn't immediately communicate what you do and who you serve; there's no clear prominent call to action above the fold; the site loads slowly on mobile; social proof is absent or buried; and the contact process has too many steps. Any one of these issues can destroy conversions even if everything else is strong.
Does website speed really affect conversions?

Significantly. Google data shows a 1-second delay in mobile page load time reduces conversions by up to 20%. For every additional second a page takes to load, the probability of a bounce increases by roughly 32%. Most Irish SME websites score poorly on PageSpeed Insights — this is one of the easiest wins available and also affects your Google search ranking.
How long does it take to see results after improving conversions?

Unlike SEO (which takes months), conversion rate improvements show results immediately. If you fix your call to action, improve your headline, and simplify your contact form — the very next visitor to your site benefits. For a site with any meaningful traffic, even a 1% improvement in conversion rate can mean 10–30 additional enquiries per month.
Should I focus on more traffic or better conversions first?

Always fix conversions first. If your site converts at 1% and you double your traffic, you get double the leads — but you've paid twice as much for the same result. If instead you double your conversion rate from 1% to 2% using the same traffic, you've doubled your leads for free. Once your site converts well, every pound or euro spent on traffic has twice the ROI.
