Why Irish Tradespeople Are
Invisible on Google
(And the 3-Step Fix)

May 1, 2026 By Vornela Systems

Search "electrician Dublin" right now. The three businesses that appear in that map pack at the top of the results are collecting the majority of all inbound enquiries for that search. Below them, organic results fight for scraps. Below page one, nothing.

The tradespeople in that map pack aren't necessarily the best in the city. They're not the cheapest, or the longest-established. They're there because they did three specific things that most of their competitors haven't bothered with. And the businesses that aren't showing up — no matter how good their work — are effectively invisible to thousands of people searching for exactly what they offer every month.

This is the single most fixable problem in Irish tradespeople's marketing, and it costs businesses tens of thousands of euro in work every year.

The Scale of the Opportunity You're Missing

Consider the search volume for trade-related queries in Ireland each month:

  • "Electrician Dublin" — over 1,300 searches per month
  • "Plumber Cork" — over 800 searches per month
  • "Roofer near me" — hundreds of searches monthly across every county
  • "Builder Galway", "tiler Limerick", "landscaper Wicklow" — each generating consistent, high-intent search volume

These aren't people browsing. They have a problem right now and they are actively looking for someone to solve it. They will call the first business that looks credible enough. The question is whether that business is yours.

The critical data point: 87% of consumers research a business online before contacting — even when they were referred by a friend. A personal recommendation no longer bypasses Google; it feeds straight into it. People Google the name they were just given.

Why You're Not Appearing (It's Not What You Think)

The most common assumption is that Google favours the businesses that have been around the longest, or that have the flashiest website, or that have paid for ads. None of these are the primary factors in local search ranking.

Google's local ranking algorithm comes down to three things: relevance (does your listing match the search?), distance (are you physically nearby?), and prominence (does Google have evidence that you're a credible, active business?). You can't change your distance, but relevance and prominence are almost entirely within your control.

The three reasons most Irish tradespeople don't appear:

  1. They haven't claimed or completed their Google Business Profile
  2. They have few or no reviews (or haven't received one in months)
  3. Their listing is dormant — no photos added, no posts, no updates

That's it. Fix those three things, and you will outrank the majority of your local competitors within weeks.

Step 1: Claim and Complete Your Google Business Profile

Go to business.google.com and search for your business. If you haven't claimed it, Google may have auto-generated a listing for you — incomplete and unverified. Claim it. If it doesn't exist, create it.

Once you're in, complete every single field. Most businesses fill in the basics and leave it there. That's a mistake. Google rewards completeness because it signals a legitimate, active business. Here's what to fill in:

  • Business name — your exact trading name, no keyword stuffing (Google penalises this)
  • Category — be specific. "Electrician" not "Contractor". You can add secondary categories too
  • Address or service area — if you travel to customers, use the service area option rather than a fixed address
  • Phone number — your primary contact number, consistent with what's on your website
  • Website URL — if you don't have one yet, this is even more reason to build one
  • Opening hours — accurate and up to date, including holiday adjustments
  • Business description — 750 characters maximum. Include your primary service, location, years in business, and what makes you different. Write for the customer, not for Google
  • Services — add each service individually with a short description. This dramatically improves relevance matching

Quick win: Add at least 10 photos immediately after claiming your profile. Google's data shows that businesses with 10+ photos receive 35% more website clicks and 42% more requests for directions than those with fewer. Use real job photos — before and afters, on-site shots, finished work.

Step 2: Build a Review Engine (Not a One-Off Ask)

Reviews are the single most powerful ranking signal in local SEO. Not just the number — the recency, the consistency, and whether you respond to them.

Most tradespeople have somewhere between zero and eight reviews. The ones dominating the map pack have 40, 60, 80 or more — and they're getting new ones regularly. The gap feels enormous, but it's more achievable than it looks if you treat reviews as a system rather than a happy accident.

How to ask without it being awkward

The best moment to ask for a review is immediately after you've completed a job and the customer has expressed satisfaction. This is when goodwill is highest and the memory is fresh.

The most effective method is a direct WhatsApp message rather than email. Here's a template that works:

"Hi [Name], great working with you on the [job] today. If you were happy with the work, I'd really appreciate a quick Google review — it helps us a lot. Here's the direct link: [link]. Takes about 30 seconds. Thanks, [your name]"

The Google review link is generated from your Business Profile dashboard — it goes straight to the review window, removing all friction. Save it as a text shortcut on your phone so you can send it within minutes of finishing a job.

The target: 2 to 4 reviews per month

At that pace, within six months you'll have between 12 and 24 recent reviews — enough to outrank the majority of competitors who stopped actively requesting them years ago. Within a year, you're building the kind of review volume that makes your profile look genuinely authoritative.

Step 3: Keep the Profile Active

Google treats an active, regularly updated profile as a trust signal. A profile that hasn't changed in two years looks abandoned, even if the business is busy. Activity signals legitimacy.

The three things that matter most for ongoing activity:

Post job photos weekly

Every completed job is content. Take 2 to 3 photos before you leave the site — the finished work, any standout detail, a before-and-after if relevant. Upload them to your Google Business Profile the same day. These photos appear directly in your listing and in Maps results. Businesses that add photos regularly receive significantly more profile views than those that don't.

Respond to every review

Every review — positive and negative — deserves a response. For positive reviews, a short, genuine thank-you is enough. For negative reviews (which happen to everyone eventually), a professional, solution-focused response demonstrates to future customers that you take your work seriously. Google also factors in response rate as a signal of business quality.

Post updates monthly

Google Business Profile lets you post updates, offers, and announcements that appear directly in your listing. A monthly post — a job highlight, a seasonal service reminder, a customer story — keeps your profile looking current and gives Google fresh content to index.

What Happens After You Fix These Three Things

Within 2 to 4 weeks of completing your profile and adding photos, you'll typically see an increase in profile views and phone calls tracked through the GBP dashboard. Within 3 months of consistent review-building and posting, most businesses see a measurable improvement in where they appear for local searches.

One client of ours — a plumber in County Clare who had three reviews and an incomplete profile — went from invisible to the second result in the local map pack within 11 weeks of implementing these steps. His inbound enquiries through Google tripled. He did nothing else differently.

The businesses winning in local search aren't spending money on ads. They're doing the basics consistently — and their competitors aren't.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get my trade business to show up on Google Maps in Ireland?

Claim and fully complete your Google Business Profile at business.google.com. Add your service area, phone number, website, business category, opening hours, a keyword-rich description, and at least 10 photos. Once verified, your business becomes eligible to appear in Google Maps results for local searches relevant to your trade.
Is Google Business Profile free?

Yes — completely free. There is no cost to create or maintain a Google Business Profile. It is one of the highest-ROI marketing activities available to any Irish tradesperson because it directly determines whether you appear in local search results when someone nearby is looking for your services.
How many Google reviews do I need to rank locally?

There's no fixed number, but businesses with 40 or more reviews tend to rank significantly better than those with fewer than 10. Recency also matters — a profile with 15 recent reviews will often outrank one with 50 older ones. Aim for 2 to 4 new reviews per month rather than trying to collect them all at once.
Does a tradesperson need a website if they already have a Google Business Profile?

A Google Business Profile is essential, but it has limits — Google controls it, it can be suspended, and it doesn't let you tell your full story or capture leads on your own terms. A website works alongside your GBP: Google sends people to your profile, but a website gives them the depth and trust signals that turn browsers into actual enquiries.
How long does it take to see results from local SEO?

For Google Business Profile improvements, you can start seeing increased visibility within 2 to 4 weeks of completing your profile and adding fresh photos. Significant ranking improvements for competitive keywords like "plumber Dublin" typically take 3 to 6 months of consistent activity. The businesses that win are those that treat their GBP as a live channel, not a set-and-forget listing.
