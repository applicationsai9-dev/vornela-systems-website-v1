Google Business Profile Ireland:
How to Set It Up and
Get Found in 2026

April 10, 2026 By Vornela Systems

Google Business Profile is the free tool that puts your Irish business on Google Maps and in local search results when someone nearby searches for what you offer. It costs nothing, takes a day to set up properly, and is almost certainly the single highest-return action most Irish small businesses aren't doing right.

According to the dot.ie Digital Health Index, 1 in 6 Irish SMEs still have no online presence whatsoever. Of the ones that do, the majority have incomplete Google profiles sitting with two photos and no reviews, wondering why competitors with busier-looking listings are getting all the calls.

This guide tells you exactly what to do — set up, optimise, and stay active — so your business shows up when customers in your area are searching.

What Google Business Profile Actually Does

When someone in Ireland searches "plumber near me" or "electrician Dublin", Google shows a map pack — three local businesses highlighted at the top of the results page. These three positions receive the majority of all clicks and calls from that search. The organic results below them get significantly less traffic. Everything below page one gets almost none.

Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is what determines whether your business appears in that map pack. It's a free listing that includes your business name, address or service area, phone number, opening hours, photos, customer reviews, services, and a link to your website. When it's complete, active, and well-reviewed, Google includes you in local results for relevant searches. When it's incomplete or dormant, you're invisible.

57% of web traffic in Ireland is now mobile (StatCounter, 2026). Most local searches happen on a phone, often urgently — someone's boiler has broken, a pipe has burst, they need a quote today. The businesses that appear first get those calls. Google Business Profile is what gets you there.

How to Set Up Your Google Business Profile in Ireland

Go to business.google.com and sign in with a Google account. Search for your business name. If Google has already auto-generated a listing (which it often does from publicly available data), claim it. If nothing exists, create a new listing from scratch.

You'll be asked to verify ownership — usually via a postcard sent to your business address with a code, or via phone/email for eligible businesses. Once verified, work through every section of the profile:

Business name

Use your exact trading name only. Do not add keywords to your name ("Joe Murphy Plumbing — Emergency Plumber Dublin") — Google penalises keyword stuffing in business names and it can result in your listing being suspended.

Primary category

This is the most important field for relevance matching. Be specific: "Electrician" not "Contractor", "Roofer" not "Construction Company", "Web Design Agency" not "Marketing Agency". You can add secondary categories too — add every one that legitimately applies to your business.

Address or service area

If customers come to you, add your address. If you travel to customers (most tradespeople), use the service area option instead — list every county and city you cover. Don't add an address if you don't want it public.

Description

750 characters. Write this for the customer, not for Google. Say what you do, where you do it, how long you've been doing it, and what makes you different. Include your primary trade/service and your main counties or cities naturally — not stuffed, just mentioned as part of who you are.

Services

Add each service individually with a short description. This dramatically improves how well Google matches you to relevant searches. A plumber who lists "Boiler Installation", "Leak Repair", "Bathroom Fitting", and "Central Heating" separately will appear for all four searches; one who just says "Plumbing" will match fewer.

Photos

Add a minimum of 10 photos immediately. Google's own data shows businesses with 10+ photos receive 35% more website clicks and 42% more requests for directions than those with fewer. Use real photos: job photos, before-and-afters, your van, your team. Customers are looking for evidence you're real and that your work is good.

The Review Strategy That Actually Works

Reviews are the highest-weighted factor in local search ranking after completeness and proximity. The businesses at the top of the map pack in your area almost certainly have more reviews, more recent reviews, and higher average ratings than the ones below them.

The problem most business owners have with reviews isn't willingness — it's system. They plan to ask, forget, remember when it's too late, and end up with eight reviews from three years ago. A system fixes this.

The method that works: The moment a job is done and the customer is happy, send a WhatsApp message with your Google review link. "Hi [Name], great working with you. If you're happy with the job, a quick Google review would really help us out — here's the direct link: [link]. Takes about 30 seconds." The link goes directly to the review window — no navigating required. Save it as a text shortcut so you can send it in under 10 seconds.

The target is 2 to 4 new reviews per month. At that pace, you'll have 24 to 48 fresh reviews within a year — enough to outrank the majority of competitors in most Irish towns and cities. Respond to every review, positive and negative. Google weighs response rate as a trust signal.

Staying Active: What Google Actually Rewards

An active profile signals to Google that your business is genuine and current. A profile that hasn't changed since 2022 looks abandoned, even if the business is thriving.

Three habits that keep your profile working:

Post job photos weekly

Every completed job is content. Take 2–3 photos before you leave the site. Upload them to your GBP the same day. Photos with location-tagged descriptions ("New roof installation in Sandyford, Dublin") help Google match you to geographic searches in that area.

Post updates monthly

Google Business Profile lets you post updates — a job highlight, a seasonal reminder, a promotion — that appear directly in your listing. One post per month keeps the profile looking current and gives Google fresh content to index.

Answer every question on your listing

Anyone can add a question to your Google listing. Check regularly and answer promptly — unanswered questions create doubt in potential customers and missed opportunities to add keyword-rich content to your profile.

Common Mistakes Irish Businesses Make

  • Leaving the category as "Local Business" — too broad to match any specific search
  • Using a landline as the primary number — Google prioritises mobile-reachable businesses for mobile searches
  • Not setting service areas — if you travel to customers and haven't set a service area, you'll only appear for searches in your immediate location
  • Ignoring negative reviews — one unanswered bad review damages trust more than five positive ones repair it
  • Adding a fake address — Google's verification process will catch this, and it can result in permanent suspension

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I set up Google Business Profile in Ireland?

Go to business.google.com and sign in with a Google account. Search for your business — if it exists, claim it. If not, create a new listing. Verify ownership via postcard or phone/email, then complete every section: business category, description, services, hours, photos, and website URL.
Is Google Business Profile free in Ireland?

Yes, completely free. There is no cost to create, verify, or maintain a Google Business Profile. It's one of the highest-return actions available to any Irish business — costing nothing but time — and it directly determines whether your business appears when someone nearby searches for your services.
How long does it take to appear on Google Maps after setting up a profile?

After verification, your business typically appears in Google Maps within a few days. Appearing prominently in the local map pack for competitive searches takes longer — usually 4 to 12 weeks of consistent activity including photos, reviews, and regular posts. The businesses at the top of the map pack have maintained their profiles consistently over time, not just set them up.
Why is my business not showing up on Google Maps in Ireland?

The most common reasons are: your profile is unclaimed or incomplete, you have very few recent reviews, your profile hasn't been updated in a long time, or your business category doesn't match the search term. Google ranks local businesses on relevance, distance, and prominence — improve relevance by completing your category and services list, and prominence by gathering reviews and posting regularly.
How many Google reviews do I need to rank locally in Ireland?

There's no fixed number, but profiles with 40 or more recent reviews consistently rank better than those with fewer than 10. Recency matters as much as volume — a business with 15 reviews from the last 3 months will often outrank one with 60 reviews from two years ago. Aim for 2 to 4 new reviews per month.
